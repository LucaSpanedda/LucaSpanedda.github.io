Biography



Luca Spanedda was born in Rome on 15th February 1995.
Since when he was a child has developed an extreme fascination for music.
He experienced as disk jockey of some jungle collectives in Rome,
produced and published some LPs and he wrote a piece of music for the RAI film "Aquadro".
He graduated in economics, finance and marketing in 2015,
consequently beginning his academic program in electronic music at the Santa Cecilia Conservatory of Rome,
as a student of electroacoustic composition with the masters Michelangelo Lupone and Nicola Bernardini.
In 2017 he presented "Asteroidi" an audio-visual work,
with the multimedia composition class of M. Riccardo Santoboni at the Master in Sonic Arts
(University of Rome Tor Vergata)
and at the Academic Hall of the Conservatory Santa Cecilia.
In 2021, he performed in concert the electronic part
of the Post-prae-ludium n. 1 for Donau by Luigi Nono,
at the Goethe-Institut Italien in Rome, ArteScienza festival.
Also in 2021 he performs live the LP released by Mossa Records
(independent Roman electronic music label), at the Klang club in Rome,
and writes a song for Giovanni La Gorga's film: From My House In Da House.
He is now enrolled in the last two-year course (Biennio)
of Electronic Music at the Conservatory Santa Cecilia in Rome.
