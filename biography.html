Biography

Luca Spanedda is a Composer and Musician born in Rome on 15th February 1995. His musical attention passes from cybernetics to technologies, and from sound and human spaces to social criticism. Graduated from high school in 2015 with specialization in: Economics, Finance and Marketing, consequently beginning his academic program in Electronic Music at the Santa Cecilia Conservatory of Rome, as a student of electroacoustic composition with the masters: Michelangelo Lupone and Nicola Bernardini. and Graduated in 2023 with the vote of 110/110 cum laude in the Master's degree in Electronic Music at the Santa Cecilia Conservatory in Rome, with a Thesis on Complex Adaptive Systems for the performance in Live Electronics, in collaboration with supervisor Giuseppe Silvi and co-supervisors Agostino Di Scipio and Dario Sanfilippo. He started making music as a self-taught musician in teenage year, doing the DJ and the Producer in some Drum and Bass collectives in Rome, and publishing some LPs. He wrote some pieces of music for films: the RAI film "Aquadro" - 2013, From My House In Da House a Giovanni La Gorga's film - 2021. In 2017 he presented "Asteroidi" an audio-visual work, with the multimedia composition class of M. Riccardo Santoboni at the Master in Sonic Arts (University of Rome Tor Vergata) and at the Academic Hall of the Conservatory Santa Cecilia. In 2021, he performed in concert the electronic part of the Post-prae-ludium n. 1 for Donau by Luigi Nono, at the Goethe-Institut Italien in Rome, ArteScienza festival. And also in 2021 he performed live the LP released by Mossa Records (independent Roman electronic music label), at the Klang club in Rome. In 2022 his piece Wires (For String Quintet) was performed at the Sala Medaglioni of the Conservatory Santa Cecilia.
